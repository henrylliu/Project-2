Proposition: Does a country's economic strength play a role in CO2 emissions?

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Picked top 3 and bottom 3 countries in gross GDP in today's standards
  • Originally wished to pick top and bottom countries in GDP, but would appear as if the data was extremely cherry-picked, so showed more countries to show this trend is for multiple countries
  • Design choices: Top emitters were more of a warning color, using colors such as orange, yellow, and pink to show that this is in a sense the “danger area” for CO2. We tried avoiding red and green in consideration of the colorblind.

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Used diff year (x label) ranges since it made the point clearer - also, different income countries tended to have spikes in GDP around different times.
  • Dual axes are actually on the same scale, which made the point clearer.
  • Top 3 Countries: USA, Germany, Japan, Bottom 3 Countries: Marshall Island, Tuvalu, Kiribati, Used the mean of the top (gross GDP), and mean of the bottom (Gross GDP)
